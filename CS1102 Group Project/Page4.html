Cryptocurrency Transactions

Cryptocurrency is digital currency which does not need to be verified by a financial institution or central bank. Rather, blockchain technology is generating an immutable ledger that records transactions and the acquisition of digital assets, used to validate and record this virtual currency. Regardless of your interest in using them for investment or for professional purposes, you can know how cryptocurrencies operate is a crucial first step. Bitcoin Transactions

Bitcoin transactions, which enable users to send and receive digital currency securely, are the backbone of the Bitcoin network. Here is about how a Bitcoin transaction is done:

  1. Initiation: A user initiates a transaction by using their Bitcoin wallet to enter the recipient's Bitcoin address and the amount to be sent.
  2. Signing: Confirming their ownership of the funds, signed with the sender's private key.
  3. Broadcasting: Where it is picked up by miners, The previous step is broadcasted to the Bitcoin network.
  4. Verification: By reviewing the sender's balance and assuring the transaction adheres to the network's rules, miners verify the transaction.
  5. Inclusion in a Block: The transaction will included in a block and combined to the blockchain, leading it to change to permanent and immutable when it is verified.
  6. Completion: When the transaction is confirmed, the recipient wallet reflects the received amount.

Wallets and Addresses: How They Work

A Bitcoin wallet is a software application. There are serveral wallet, each wallet contains unique identifiers, which is one or more addresses. Theses unique identifiers may serve as the recipient's online destination for transactions. Totally speaking, Bitcoin wallet allows users to store, send, and receive Bitcoin.

  • Public Address: Can be shared with others to receive funds, similar as email address.
  • Private Key: A secret key, anyone with it can control the associated funds, so it must be kept secure as it allows owner to access and manage their Bitcoin.

Comparative Analysis of Cryptocurrencies

Overview of Popular Cryptocurrencies

  1. Bitcoin (BTC): The first and most widely recognized cryptocurrency, known for its decentralized nature and fixed supply.
  2. Ethereum (ETH): A platform for building decentralized applications (dApps) and smart contracts, enabling programmable transactions.
  3. Litecoin (LTC): Created as a "lighter" version of Bitcoin, it offers faster transaction speeds and a different hashing algorithm.
